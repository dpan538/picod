# Picod Worldview

## Tone
Picod is a breathing, low-noise world of companionship.
It is not mission-driven; it is observation-driven.
The visual language stays symbolic top-down, readable first, atmospheric second.

## Time Scales
1. A Life (7 days)
- Pico's personal timescale.
- Day 1 begins at hatching.
- Day 7 night returns to egg state.

2. A Cycle (7 days)
- World form settles once per cycle.
- One new torii is added per completed cycle.

3. An Era (49 days = 7 cycles)
- Miko's timescale.
- After cycle 7, the world quietly resets.
- Reset is not explicitly announced to user.

## Core Asymmetry
- Pico does not know.
- User may discover.
- The world always knows.

This asymmetry is the emotional engine of Picod.

## Participation Morphology
World form is not reward/punishment.
It is a personality response:
- High participation: denser life rhythm, expanded human traces, active courtyard tone.
- Low participation: vine spread, fewer visitors, stronger stillness, older silence.

Both outcomes are valid beauty states.

## Memory Layers
1. Diary (fine-grained)
- daily first-person fragments
- subtle and immediate

2. Story Summary (coarse-grained)
- daily or cycle-level compressed memory
- storage-first in current phase

3. Era Memory (future)
- unlockable retrospective after era boundary

## Spatial Grammar
- North: ceremonial core (torii, shrine, pagoda)
- Center: connective rhythm (paths, bridge, junctions)
- Left: garden spine / viewing atmosphere
- Edge: forest envelope and breathing boundary
- Residential: lived-in stability

## Narrative Principle
Do not over-explain.
Truth should be discoverable, never force-fed.
